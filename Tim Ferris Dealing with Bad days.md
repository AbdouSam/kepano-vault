- **Key Insights on Productivity & Busyness**:
    - Being "busy" is often a form of laziness (lazy thinking + indiscriminate action). More activity doesn't equal better results.
    - Doing something _well_ doesn't make it _important_ — prioritize the "what" over the "how."
    - Single-tasking on high-leverage items can make you _appear_ superhuman to others.
- **Tim's 8-Step Process for Maximizing Efficacy (Doing the Right Things)**:
    
    - This is his core "coping mechanism" when feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or unproductive. It focuses on **efficacy** (right things) rather than efficiency (doing things fast/well). He uses it to create big outcomes even when procrastinating or napping.
    - If he has 10 important things, nothing important gets done — so he narrows to **one must-do** and blocks protected morning time for it.
    
    1. **Wake up at least 1 hour before you have to be at a computer screen** — Avoid jumping straight into reactive mode (e.g., email is the "mind-killer").
    2. **Make a cup of tea** (e.g., pu'er tea) and sit down with pen/pencil and paper (analog, not digital).
    3. **Write down only 3–5 things making you most anxious or uncomfortable** — These are often the tasks repeatedly punted on to-do lists. The most important ones are usually the most uncomfortable (with risk of rejection or conflict).
    4. **For each item, ask two questions**:
        - If this were the _only_ thing I accomplished today, would I be satisfied with my day?
        - Will moving this forward make all the other to-dos unimportant or much easier later? (Inspired by _The One Thing_ by Gary Keller).
    5. **Narrow to the items you answered "yes" to for at least one question** — These are the high-leverage priorities.
    6. **Block out at least 2–3 uninterrupted hours today to focus on _just one_ of them**.
    7. **Protect that block fiercely** — No distractions, no social media, no multitasking. (Note: Cobbling together scattered 10-minute sessions doesn't work.)
    8. **If you get distracted or procrastinate, don't spiral** — Gently return to the one task without self-judgment.
- **Closing Encouragement**:
    - This single-tasking approach is how Tim gets big results despite his flaws.
    - When feeling far from perfect or like you're "losing at life," remember: Even the best feel this way. You're not alone — everyone has hidden battles.
    - Reference to Kurt Vonnegut: "When I write, I feel like an armless, legless man with a crayon in his mouth."
    - Don't overestimate the world and underestimate yourself — you're capable of more than you think.
