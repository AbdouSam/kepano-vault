This is a summary of the book
  
One minute a week.

A good manger give a clear goal to the team members.

A great manager tells the member what the overall goal is and let them pick the target and position themselves to best reach the target.

The idea is not to directly give the team-member a goal, but help the member to "define his own goal" in accordance with the overall direction. This is done through the one minute process.

## 1. A Weekly one-minute goal setting session:

  
"initially it will take more than one minute, but with time people will do the work upfront, so that it will take one minute"

Manager: what are you hoping to complete this week? Don't be afraid to stretch yourself.

Member: (thinking about multiple things to do this, week, mentioning few...)

Manager: of those goals, what one could have the largest impact. Using the one-thing approach and the 80/20. what is the one goal if achieved will help the team more than all the goals combined.
  
Member: (will think out loud about the most possible goal)

Manager: Can you clarify what exactly you plan to accomplish? (The idea is to get the member to explain the goal with as much details as possible)

Member: (will mention the goal with as details as possible)

Manager: Now, that you have clarified the most important goal, type out the goal with the steps you plan to accomplish your goal, keep it to less than a page something like

```yml

  

Goal: Implement redunandancy for EEPROM
   step 1: experiement with writing on eeprom...
   step 2: establish the unit tests for the big structure
   step 3: implement first version...

```

  

Manager: once you have a one-pager for this goal, email it to me so that i can approve it and review it.
After they send the email, and you review it, you approve it and you tell them
Manager: print out your goal and keep it in front of you. Every day, take a minute to ask yourself, did my actions today got me closer to completing my goal.
## 2. One minute praising

Imagine of your direct reports (as a manager) needs to learn new skill or new software to complete the task. you direct the team member to self taught tutorials and ask them to comeback to you if they have questions. This is usually a bad idea because people usually refrain from asking help. what you should do is
- Ask the team member to share the screen, and watch them using the new software, ease the ambiance by telling him/her, i am only watching to catch you doing something right. praise them when they do something good.

The one minute praising, is to
 
1. specify what they did right and praise it and tell them why is it important (to do the thing they did)
 
2. you pause and you let them feel good about it.
  
3. encourage to keep learning

  
These meetings are held early in the process, praise any progress
## 3. one minute redirect
 
This is when your direct report, knows the goal clearly and knows how to use the tools, but misses his goal. it is time for direct.

Similarly to Sports and coaches, when a team member is distracted, missing shots, not focused

the redirect is as follow, take the team member on a one to one, not in front of the others:

1. Tell them exactly what is it they are doing wrong
2. tell them how you feel about that and how the team is being affected by this.
3. remind the player that he is better than this.
  
## final thoughts

Your job as a manager is not to manage people, but to get your people to manage themselves

"No person ever really works for anyone else, deep down people like to work for themselves"
