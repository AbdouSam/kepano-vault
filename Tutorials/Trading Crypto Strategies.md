### Executable Crypto Trading Strategies

  

Based on an in-depth analysis of the posts from the specified traders, I have synthesized their approaches into a list of executable strategies. #### 1. Dip Buying in Uptrends (From @EmperorBTC, @IncomeSharks, @CryptoPoseidonn)

   - **Description**: Buy pullbacks in confirmed uptrends, using EMAs and support zones for entry. This strategy leverages institutional buying and reduced supply during dips.

   - **Entry Rules**: Enter long when price retests a key support (e.g., 50-day EMA or previous range low) with volume confirmation (OBV holding support). For BTC/ETH, bid 5-10% dips from recent highs.

   - **Exit Rules**: Take partial profits at 20-30% gain or next resistance (Fib 0.618 level). Trail stop below 20-day EMA.

   - **Risk Management**: Risk 1-2% per trade, stop-loss below support (e.g., 5% below entry). Position size based on portfolio (10-20% allocation to majors).

   - **Indicators**: 50/200 EMA crossover for trend, OBV for divergence, Volume Profile for S/R.

   - **Accuracy Example**: @IncomeSharks called ETH dips to ~4,000 on Aug 19; price hit 4,000 and bounced 20% to 4,800 by Aug 23.

  

#### 2. Range Trading with Deviations (From @trader1sz, @Roman_Trading, @BullyDCrypto)

   - **Description**: Trade within defined ranges, entering on deviations (wicks outside range) and targeting opposites. Ideal for BTC when alts like ETH trend.

   - **Entry Rules**: Long range lows on deviation wick + reclaim (e.g., BTC at 112k deviation on Aug 21). Short highs if volume weak.

   - **Exit Rules**: TP at range mid/high (50-100% of range width). Exit if clean break with volume.

   - **Risk Management**: Stop-loss outside deviation (2-3% risk). Use 5-10% position size in ranges.

   - **Indicators**: VWAP for session anchors, Volume Profile POC/VAL/VAH, trendlines for ranges.

   - **Accuracy Example**: @trader1sz called BTC range 112k-120k on Aug 12; price ranged and broke higher on Aug 23 to 117k.

  

#### 3. Breakout Trading with Volume Confirmation (From @Roman_Trading, @Nebraskangooner, @eliz883)

   - **Description**: Enter on breakouts of key levels with high volume, focusing on HTF for direction. Horizontal S/R > diagonal.

   - **Entry Rules**: Long on breakout above resistance (e.g., ETH 4,350 on Aug 21) with volume spike. Retest for confirmation.

   - **Exit Rules**: TP at next resistance (Fib extensions). Trail stop below breakout level.

   - **Risk Management**: Risk 1% per trade, stop below breakout (3-5%). Avoid news-based pumps without volume.

   - **Indicators**: Weekly/daily volume for validation, 11 EMA for bounces, RSI >50 for bull bias.

   - **Accuracy Example**: @Nebraskangooner called ETH breakout above 4,000 on Aug 11; price hit 4,878 ATH by Aug 23.

  

#### 4. Trend Following with MA and OBV (From @TraderMercury, @IncomeSharks)

# Crypto Trading Strategies from @Luckshuryy

  

## 1. Order Flow Absorption Scalping

  

- **Description**: Enter trades at key support/resistance levels where large orders are absorbed, indicating strong buyer/seller presence. Uses footprint charts to spot absorption (large market orders hitting limit orders without price movement).

- **Entry Rules**: Long when aggressive market orders (e.g., buys) hit a support level (VAL or prior low) but price holds, confirmed by footprint delta (positive for buys). Example: BTC at 112k on Aug 21, absorption signaled by clustered buy orders with minimal price drop. Short at VAH if sell orders absorbed.

- **Exit Rules**: Take profit (TP) at next POC or opposite extreme (1-2% move). Exit on delta reversal or structure break (e.g., new low). Hold for minutes to hours.

- **Risk Management**: Risk 0.5-1% per trade, stop-loss 1-2% below/above absorption level. Use 5-10% position size for scalps.

- **Indicators**: Footprint charts (delta, volume), Volume Profile (VAL/VAH/POC), DOM for real-time order flow. Tools like Exocharts or ATAS recommended.

- **Accuracy Example**: @Luckshuryy called BTC absorption at 112k on Aug 21; price held and rallied to 117k by Aug 23.

- **Notes**: Best in high-liquidity markets (BTC/ETH). Avoid during low-volume periods (e.g., Asia session).

  

## 2. Bart Pattern Reversal

  

- **Description**: Trade reversals after a "Bart pattern" (sharp move up/down due to excessive positioning, followed by quick unwind). Often seen in overextended crypto pumps/dumps.

- **Entry Rules**: Short after a sharp upward move (e.g., 5-10% in hours) with structure break (new high, then rejection) and high volume on footprint. Long at support after downward Bart. Example: BTC pump to 120k, rejection, and drop on Aug 19.

- **Exit Rules**: TP at prior low/high or Fib 0.618 retracement (2-5% move). Exit on new structure high/low.

- **Risk Management**: Risk 1%, stop above Bart high (shorts) or below low (longs). Use 10% position size.

- **Indicators**: Footprint for volume spikes, Volume Profile for key levels, RSI for overbought/oversold.

- **Accuracy Example**: @Luckshuryy highlighted Bart pattern on Aug 14; BTC dropped from 120k to 112k after rejection.

- **Notes**: Works best on 5-15m charts. Confirm with order flow (e.g., sell-off after buy exhaustion).

  

## 3. Market Profile Breakout

  

- **Description**: Trade breakouts from high-volume nodes (HVNs) or low-volume nodes (LVNs) using market profile to identify liquidity zones. Focus on directional moves post-consolidation.

- **Entry Rules**: Long on breakout above HVN (e.g., BTC 115k on Aug 15) with strong buy volume on DOM/footprint. Short below LVN with sell volume confirmation. Wait for retest.

- **Exit Rules**: TP at next HVN or Fib extension (1.618). Exit on failed retest or volume drop.

- **Risk Management**: Risk 1-2%, stop below breakout level (2-3%). Allocate 10-15% to majors.

- **Indicators**: Market Profile (HVN/LVN), DOM, Volume Profile, delta on footprint.

- **Accuracy Example**: @Luckshuryy called ETH breakout above 4,350 on Aug 11; price hit 4,878 by Aug 23.[](https://www.cryptowisser.com/guides/crypto-order-flow-analysis-guide)

- **Notes**: Use 1h/daily for bias, 5m for entries. Avoid breakouts during news volatility.

  

## 4. Liquidity Grab and Reversal

  

- **Description**: Trade reversals after price grabs liquidity (wicks beyond S/R) to trap traders, using order flow to confirm institutional intent.

- **Entry Rules**: Long after price wicks below support (e.g., BTC 112k on Aug 19) but buy orders absorb on footprint. Short after wick above resistance with sell absorption.

- **Exit Rules**: TP at prior high/low or next S/R (2-4% move). Exit on new wick or delta flip.

- **Risk Management**: Risk 0.5-1%, stop beyond wick (1-2%). Use 5-10% position size.

- **Indicators**: Footprint (delta for absorption), Volume Profile (S/R), DOM for order clustering.

- **Accuracy Example**: @Luckshuryy noted BTC liquidity grab at 112k on Aug 19; price reversed to 117k.

- **Notes**: Best on 15m-1h charts. Confirm with on-chain data (e.g., whale buys on Dexscreener).

  

## 5. VWAP-Anchored Scalping

  

- **Description**: Scalp around VWAP (session or daily) as a dynamic S/R, using order flow to confirm entries. Popular for intraday crypto volatility.

- **Entry Rules**: Long at VWAP support (e.g., ETH 4,200 on Aug 20) with buy delta on footprint. Short at VWAP resistance with sell delta.

- **Exit Rules**: TP at 1-2% move or next S/R. Exit on VWAP break or delta reversal.

- **Risk Management**: Risk 0.5%, tight stop (1%). Use 5% position size for scalps.

- **Indicators**: VWAP, footprint delta, Volume Profile (POC/VAL/VAH).

- **Accuracy Example**: @Luckshuryy’s VWAP scalp call on ETH Aug 20; price bounced from 4,200 to 4,300.

- **Notes**: Focus on London/NY sessions. Avoid choppy markets.
