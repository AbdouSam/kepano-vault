### The market Cycle

  

This is also inspired by ICT, which are Expansion, Cumulation, retracting,...t

The Market usually starts at a Fair value, the price is in Balance, buyers and sellers are happy to buy/sell at this level, price is stable.

  

Then, the trigger occurs a "Market Imbalance" that is characterized by either :

- Demand > Supply -> Price goes up

- Supply > Demand -> Price goes down

  

The price will fall and rise until it reaches a new fair value zone.

  

![[Pasted image 20250830131502.png]]

  

LTF ranging from 1m to 1h TF, usually markets range 70% - 80% of the time, and this range is the fair value, where buyers/sellers are at equilibrium

  

From this perspective there are two types of Traders:

- **HTF Traders** : they create orders in the order book, and they leave the orders open to be filled in long term (HTF, hours, days,...), typically these are big size orders

- **LTF Traders** : these traders uses usually smaller sizes, but quickly executed put in the orderbook or removed in matter of minutes

The price will range and try to reach for the HTF orders, but it get consumed by LTF orders and reverse.

This will continue until the cumulation of LTF orders exceeds the HTF orders and in opposite direction of the HTF are removed, order cancelled for some reasons

![[Pasted image 20250830132301.png]]

one can see the HTF put orders in the red zone, the LTF are trading on the block dots.

the first time the LTF orders where so aggressive it reversed the price direction. Between the Range high and the Range low, the LTF traders controls the price movement

  

This cycles is continuous and never stops, Range, cumulation, expansion, retracement, Reversal

## Trading Support and Resistance

  

What causes a certain level to be support or resistance is several reasons.

- One of them is Market memory, if the price reverses at a particular time several levels, traders remembers that level

- Another reason is institutional interest, Institutions pick a certain level to buy/sell, these orders are large and are placed at a specific price where they see it as fair value.

- Institutions also use known levels, like previous high and low, to **absorb liquidity**

- another reason is trapped trader phenomena, this is were traders are caught in the wrong side of the market and are more than happy to exit at break-even.

- These level also have lots of volume because usually STP are put very close to these levels

## S/R characteristics

  

- S/R, revers roles frequently from S <-> R

- the levels are not just significant in terms of Price level but also in terms of occurence and Time, for example:

    - Swing high/low: Clear peaks are resistance, troughs = support or Major turning point

    - Yearly/Monthly/Weekly and Daily OPEN/CLOSE

    - High Volume points, these areas adds confluence to the resistance/support selection

    - Volume profiles

    - Psychological levels such as ALL Timer Highs are important

  

Tips:

- Levels are not precise lines, but they must be treated as guides to increase the probability, when the price reaches level, take it as a cue to pay attention, ask your self,

    - is there any confirmation of a reversal, bullish candle

    - what is the volume doing

    - is there momentum prior to the level, where the level might be broken

    - when the price reaches the level you must be prepared for both directions, will it break and bounce back
